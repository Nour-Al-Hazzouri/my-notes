# Q&A Session
## Understanding the Value of Connections

**Q: When you started your journey in dev, did you know how powerful connections are?**

**A:** No, I thought if I had powerful coding skills that would be enough. It started when clients debated me on $400-500 per project, while others with basic skills charged $5,000 for basic WordPress. It's all about marketing power.

---

**Q: How did you build your first connection?**

**A:** Funny story—it started when I sent my CV as a text file. They thought I was a geek and got accepted. It's about differentiating yourself from others and standing out.

---

**Q: Ever felt a connection of yours opened a door that created an opportunity for you?**

**A:** Yes, I had a friend who was very social and had so many connections that brought me 10+ years of projects and so many other opportunities. We all have this person in our network; we just need to know them.

---

## What Networking Really Means

**Q: Many people think networking is just adding people on LinkedIn. What does it mean to you?**

**A:** It's about building a genuine connection. Some might have 100 connections and only 2 talk to them. Building connections is better through:

1. Face-to-face
2. Video calls
3. Voice calls
4. Text

Likes and connections are last. For me, thinking networking is just LinkedIn is actually anti-social.

---

**Q: What's the most common misconception for developers about networking?**

**A:** Networking is not about getting something from somebody. It's not about just needing something—it's about providing value for the connection. The key is: **give before you take**.

---

## Communities and Contribution

**Q: What do you think about joining communities like TechTalks?**

**A:** In the networking aspect, yes, definitely this helps. Somebody might connect you to someone who's offering a job, so it might be helpful. Assuming we have 100 people, I now know 100 persons, and with enough work, you can build connections with them.

---

**Q: What's the difference between being in a community and actively contributing to it?**

**A:** Being in a community is just like LinkedIn, so contributing is important to be noticed. Always keep giving value without expecting anything. Don't enter just to get value or something. You need to talk and express yourself. You might think you don't have anything to say as a junior, but this isn't always the case. Never think your opinion isn't worth it.

---

**Q: How can students turn community collaborations into internships?**

**A:** When constantly posting, sharing, and providing value, people will notice you and opportunities will knock on your door.

---

## Platform Strategy

**Q: Which platform should devs mostly focus on?**

**A:** There are a lot, and every platform works, but for me, I'd go to places that aren't competitive. LinkedIn will have more applications than other platforms. Contributing to open source is good, posting on LinkedIn, and staying connected. **Choose one platform, stick with it, and conquer it.**

---

**Q: What kind of personal brand should a dev build?**

**A:** Know your strengths and weaknesses and embrace them. No one talks about their struggles. Keep posting regularly and do videos so people can see body language. When you're good at one thing, keep posting about it.

---

## Quality Over Quantity

**Q: Do you prefer more contacts or fewer strong relations?**

**A:** Strong relations will always be better than a bigger number of contacts. For example, having 3 good connections with recruiters will make finding a job easier than having 100 contacts only.

---

**Q: What's your advice for reaching out to people in companies naturally?**

**A:** Always be genuine, natural, and bold. They rarely get to see this, and they love it. You need to talk in a way where you are yourself and just let it go.

---

**Q: What's the best way to keep connections alive?**

**A:** Let's say I did an internship and met someone and exchanged contacts with them. Use software that will always remind you of who you want to stay connected with and keep in touch with them.

---

## For Introverts and Beginners

**Q: How do students build connections if they are more introverted?**

**A:** For me, it's practice. Start by standing with people that are talking, and step by step keep practicing until you're more comfortable dealing with people. Don't compare yourself to extroverts.

---

**Q: If I struggle at events where most people are not on the same ground as I am (like I'm a beginner and they are pros), how can I build connections?**

**A:** Actually talking about this would help. For example, if you're a second-year student, you can share your struggles with them and ask what they might do in your place. A lot of people love these kinds of questions and will be kind enough to explain. You might ask them if it's okay to connect with them.

---

## Networking Habits of Successful Developers

**Q: What networking habits do successful developers have that are usually ignored?**

**A:** Reach out when you don't need to, because then you will not be wanting something. You will be really building connections, and later when you actually need something, you would have a connection ready to reach out to.

---

## The Bigger Picture

**Q: Are soft skills more important than technical skills?**

**A:** Might be even more important. With the help of AI, even non-technical people can do some coding, even if not like real devs. So the human aspect is becoming more important.

---

**Q: How do you crawl from junior to senior?**

**A:** Assuming you have the skills, your connections will help you showcase your skills to get you promoted from junior to senior.

---

**Q: How can networking help startups?**

**A:** Having the best code with no one knowing about it won't reach anyone. Having someone to be a co-founder and teach marketing skills will be very useful to have reach.

---

## Advice for Students

**Q: What advice do you have for students?**

**A:** Always keep a good relationship with your connections. You don't know who will be in a powerful position later and help you. College is the best way to build a bigger network and get to know more people.

---

**Q: So is entering a community the first step and the rest is up to you?**

**A:** Everyone will have to work for standing out. The community will give you the exposure you need to work in order to stand out.

---

**Q: What's one golden rule you follow when meeting new people?**

**A:** Try to build an instant bond. If I'm meeting somebody, I do research about them and ask them questions if they're someone I care about. If not, having common ground will be enough. Connecting with them and just doing something with them might later give results.

---

## LinkedIn Strategy

**Q: About LinkedIn, if I don't post frequently or only when I achieve something new (like a certificate or finished project), will the community I'm in and my achievements be enough to get me opportunities, or is sharing more of my journey more recommended?**

**A:** Sharing more is more recommended because the algorithm is designed to keep as many people on the platform as possible, so content creators are rewarded by posting regularly. Post at least twice a week, even if not capable of constantly posting, and you will have better reach.
