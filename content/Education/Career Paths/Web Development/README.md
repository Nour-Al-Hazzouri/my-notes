# Who Are These Notes For

These notes will benefit people who like to take notes a lot for anything they learn, basically having fish memory (just like me), and constantly needing a reminder of what they learned.
Mostly Covered:
- IT Related Courses
- Islamic Religion Teachings
- Web Development Roadmap and Notes
# How I Started

Around 2020–2021, I was a motivated novice wanting to learn more about computers, potentially making it my career choice instead of it being a distraction from my actual studies (which I hated so much, BTW). I decided to switch from academic high school to vocational high school majoring in IT.

Also during BT1 (my first year), due to the economic crisis, teachers went on strike which lasted 3–4 months during that school year (being literally as long as an official summer break). At that time, while I wouldn't have minded the break, contrary to before, I actually wanted to learn and was excited to continue. Instead of wasting my time, I decided to shift to online courses. And just like any other novice, YouTube was the beginning of my journey.

I started with Python, writing notes in Notepad. Later I shifted to Microsoft Word and started taking courses from Coursera (the only free source that provided paid courses with the possibility to ask for financial aid at the time).

Note that I had no mentor or anyone to guide me through my online studies. I had to rely on myself and the hope that what I was doing was correct (unfortunately, it wasn't for a long time), and just keep going on my journey.

My turning point was the scholarship provided by AMIDEAST for the Google IT Support Professional Certificate. I enjoyed it very much, but at the same time, taking notes was exhausting. At the beginning, my notes were literally transcriptions. Then they got better to be ACTUAL notes, but I still needed to pause the video, take the note, continue, do it again, etc. Until finishing took twice the time I needed to just watch the video.

# What I Realized

I kept doing this for all my courses, even until I started with my first specialized roadmap in Web Dev. But then I noticed two important things:

1. I wasn't coming back to most of my notes, which consumed a lot of my study time.
    
2. Searching Google was faster and more efficient than searching my notes (even before AI was a thing—damn, I feel old saying this).
    

And I'm the type of person who doesn't stay motivated when I feel like what I'm doing isn't beneficial. So I felt conflicted. Having the memory of a fish, and using my time inefficiently.

# What I Decided

After finishing CS50x and starting TOP (The Odin Project), I came to a very important conclusion:

I don't even need to take notes most of the time. While the case differs based on what you do (which I assume is programming), if you understand the logic and rules behind what you're doing—or at least how it works—the syntax is really just auxiliary.

TOP pinpoints what actual professional developers do: search Google for "How to center a div." A developer is essentially someone who builds logic and fixes problems. Implementing them just requires searching for the syntax, not memorizing it.

# Have I Stopped Taking Notes?

Nope. Never have, probably never will.

What I currently do is _know_ when to take notes. In most cases, it's when I need these notes to be reminded of something very specific that Google's answer won't satisfy. Such as notes for my vocational education, or religious studies (which I will not trust Google for), or lately when I find an interesting article that I might come back to but just need the point of it, not to re-read it (as I did with some of TOP's lessons).

# Conclusion

While I doubt most of you have read anything beyond the summary, I just wanted to say I would be filled with joy if I helped even just one person. I created these notes mainly for my own use, but since I had no one to rely on and learned my lesson the hard way, being there for someone will mean a lot to me.

P.S. Most of these notes were written before AI was a thing, and while now it can make clean notes, it still hallucinates, misses things, and makes mistakes. Reviewing its work and editing it as fits you is still essential until the current date (3/2026).
