# ATS CV Writing & Personal Branding Guide

## Winning Tips for Writing an ATS CV

### 1. Include Your LinkedIn Profile

Including your LinkedIn profile increases your chances of getting a job by 71%.

![[Pasted image 20251008190701.png]]

### 2. Use the Right Keywords

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) uses AI to filter CVs based on keywords. Here are the key statistics:

- Candidates worldwide only include 51% of important keywords and skills
- The average CV uses 45 keywords (this varies by job and experience level)
- More experience typically requires more keywords

**How to Find Keywords:**

- **Free Word Cloud Generators:**
    - FreeWordcloudgenerator.com
    - Monkeylearn.com
    - Wordclouds.com
- **AI Chatbots:** ChatGPT and similar tools

### 3. Include the Right Skills

**Soft Skills (Power Skills)** Soft skills are applicable to all professions and include:

- Critical thinking
- Problem solving
- Public speaking
- Professional writing
- Teamwork
- Digital literacy
- Leadership

You can either list these skills directly or let your experience highlight your abilities. Remember, soft skills can be developed over time.

**Hard Skills** These are technical skills obtained through repetition and practice, specific to your field.

### 4. Write Naturally

Avoid over-indexing your resume with keywords. Aim for approximately 80% keyword optimization—any higher and your CV will seem robotic to HR professionals. Keywords should be integrated naturally into your content. Consider working with a professional CV writer for the best results.

### 5. Include Measurable Results

Companies prefer resumes with quantifiable results because they make your value easier to understand.

**Examples:**

- "Monitored a team of 5 people, which increased teamwork and productivity"
- "Created a new marketing strategy that significantly increased branch sales"

**Note:** If you don't have specific achievements from work experience, create your own results through personal projects. Only 26% of resumes worldwide include measurable results.

**Example:** "Led a team of 5 people on a project, which improved teamwork and communication skills"

### 6. Use the XYZ Formula

![[Pasted image 20251008191830.png]]

Structure your achievements as: **Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]**

### 7. Optimal CV Length

Keep your CV between 475 and 600 words for maximum effectiveness.

![[Pasted image 20251008191955.png]]

### 8. Avoid Buzzwords and Clichés

51% of resumes use unnecessary buzzwords that detract from your message.

**Common Buzzwords to Avoid:**

- Creative
- Detail-oriented
- Responsible
- Effective
- Experienced
- Team player

**Tip:** Search "resume buzzwords" on Google and remove them from your CV.

### 9. Use First Person

It's acceptable to write your CV in first person rather than third person.

### 10. Start with Action Verbs

Begin bullet points with action verbs in the past tense, unless describing current ongoing responsibilities. Avoid "-ing" verbs and use present simple tense sparingly, only when necessary.

## Personal Branding Through Your CV

![[Pasted image 20251008192454.png]]

### Know Your Purpose

- Understand what you want to do and why you're doing it
- Have specific goals and clear reasons for your career choices

### Stay Consistent

Inconsistency creates confusion and makes you appear less serious about your career. For example, avoid patterns like posting on LinkedIn regularly, then stopping, then starting again in a cycle.

### Be Authentic

Try to be human in your communications. Don't spam professional language that sounds AI-generated in your posts and CV.

### Tailor Your CV

For different career paths or experiences, create separate CVs. For example, if you have experience in both coding and another field, each should have its own tailored CV.

### Prepare Your Pitch

Have a clear, concise elevator pitch ready for networking opportunities and interviews.

## Common Questions & Answers

**Q: Do these tips apply to all types of businesses, including high-tier companies and startups?**

A: Professional corporations typically have more rigorous hiring processes, while startups often have easier entry points. In startups, you might have opportunities for faster promotion and growth beyond a single position.

**Q: Is it okay to send an email to a company without receiving an invitation to request an internship?**

A: Absolutely! You don't need an invitation to apply. Try various options and be proactive.

- You can also approach companies in person, depending on the field and company culture.

**Q: Should I include information irrelevant to the field I'm applying for?**

A: Never. Irrelevant information will negatively affect your application.

**Q: What are the biggest mistakes people make in their CVs?**

A: Common mistakes include:

- CVs longer than 1-2 pages
- Adding images in incorrect places
- No LinkedIn link
- Poorly organized titles and sections
- Not tailoring content to the specific position
- Focusing on what interests you rather than what the HR professional cares about

**Q: Do CV layout and design matter?**

A: Not as much as you might think. Many people have been hired by major companies with very basic CV designs. Content matters more than aesthetics.
