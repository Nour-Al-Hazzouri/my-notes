# Week 1: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

## Introducing AI

### Definition and Purpose
- **AI Definition**: By IBM's definition, anything that makes machines act more intelligently
- **Augmented Intelligence**: Shouldn't replace human experts, but extend their capabilities
- **Collaborative Potential**: Accomplish tasks neither humans nor AI can accomplish singlehandedly
- **Data Evolution**:
  - Distributed Computing/IoT led to massive amounts of data
  - Social networking encouraged unstructured data
- **Benefits for Experts**:
  - Information at experts' fingertips, backed with evidence
  - Experts scale capabilities while machines do time-consuming work

### Understanding Intelligence
- Humans have innate intelligence that governs every activity in their bodies

### How AI Learns
- Machines only have the innate intelligence we give them
- Machines examine examples to create machine learning models based on inputs and desired outputs
- **Learning Approaches**:
  - Supervised Learning
  - Unsupervised Learning
  - Reinforcement Learning

### AI Classification
- **By Strength**:

| Type                      | Characteristics                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Weak/Narrow AI**        | • Applied to specific domains (translators, virtual assistants, self-driving cars)<br>• Performs specific tasks, cannot learn new ones<br>• Makes decisions based on programmed algorithms and training data |
| **Strong/Generalized AI** | • Interacts with a wide variety of independent/unrelated tasks<br>• Learns new tasks to solve new problems<br>• Combination of many AI strategies that learn from experience                                 |
| **Super/Conscious AI**    | • AI with human-level consciousness, requiring self-awareness<br>• Not likely created in the near future as consciousness is not yet defined                                                                 |

- **Also Classified By**:
  - Breadth
  - Application

### Multidisciplinary Field
- Fusion of many fields of study:
  - Computer science and software engineering determine implementation in hardware/software
  - Mathematics and statistics determine viable models and measure performance
  - Philosophy provides guidance on philosophical and ethical considerations

## Generative AI Overview and Use Cases

### What is Generative AI?
- A type of AI technique that can generate new and unique data:
  - Images
  - Music
  - Text
  - Entire virtual worlds
- Uses deep learning techniques
- Relies on large datasets to generate new data
- **Large Language Models (LLMs)**: Type of AI using deep learning to process and generate natural language
  - Can develop powerful new LLM algorithms or architectures
  - Can be incorporated into larger, more advanced AI systems

### Significance of Generative AI
- **Key Benefits**:
  - Creativity and innovation
  - Cost and time savings
  - Personalization
  - Scalability
  - Robustness
  - Exploration of new possibilities

### Use Cases of Generative AI

#### Healthcare and Precision Medicine
- Identify genetic mutations
- Provide personalized treatment options
- Generate medical images, simulate surgeries, predict new drug properties
- Help doctors practice procedures and develop treatments

#### Agriculture
- Optimize crop yields
- Develop new, more resistant crop varieties

#### Biotechnology
- Development of new drugs and therapies by:
  - Identifying potential drug targets
  - Simulating drug interactions
  - Forecasting drug efficacy

#### Forensics
- Analyze DNA evidence
- Identify suspects

#### Environmental Conservation
- Support the protection of endangered species
- Analyze genetic data
- Suggest breeding and conservation strategies

#### Creative Industries
- Produce unique digital art, music, and video content for:
  - Advertising and marketing campaigns
  - Films or video games

#### Gaming
- Create interactive game worlds
- Generate new levels, characters, and objects in real-time

#### Fashion
- Design and produce virtual try-on experiences
- Recommend personalized fashion choices
- Analyze customer behavior and preferences

#### Robotics
- Design new robot movements
- Adapt them to changing environments
- Enable them to perform complex tasks

#### Education
- Create customized learning materials
- Develop interactive learning environments
- Adapt to learning styles and learner's pace

#### Data Augmentation
- Produce new training data for machine learning models
- Enhance learning data accuracy and performance

### Recap
- Generative AI creates new and unique data
- Outperforms traditional AI in creativity, cost/time savings, personalization, scalability, robustness
- Has potential to transform various industries and generate previously impossible data and experiences
- Can perform a wide range of tasks, similar to human intelligence

## Impact and Examples of AI

### Economic Impact
- According to PWC, AI will add 16 trillion dollars to global GDP from now until 2030

### AI Meanings Across Disciplines
- **Video Game Designer**: Code affecting how bots play or environments react to players
- **Screenwriter**: Characters that act human with computer features mixed in
- **Data Scientist**: Way of exploring and classifying data to meet specific goals

### AI Algorithms and Applications

#### Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Generation (NLG)
- Allow for human-machine interaction and create new opportunities
- **Chatbots** (powered by NLP) used in:
  - Healthcare to question patients and run basic diagnoses
  - Education to provide conversational interfaces and on-demand tutors
  - Customer service to resolve queries and free up agents' time

#### AI Speech Technology
- Real-time transcription
- Advanced speech synthesis for enhanced customer experiences
- Helps Lou Gehrig's patients regain their voices

#### Computer Vision
- Surpasses humans in detecting and labeling objects
- Detects facial features and compares with databases
- Enables authentication through facial recognition
- Helps automate:
  - Detection of cancerous moles in skin images
  - Finding symptoms in x-ray and MRI scans

#### Impact on Daily Life
- Detecting fraudulent transactions
- Identifying credit card fraud
- Preventing financial crimes

#### Healthcare Applications
- Help doctors arrive at more accurate preliminary diagnoses
- Reading medical imaging
- Finding appropriate clinical trials for patients
- Making operational processes less expensive

### AI Potential
- Access enormous amounts of information
- Imitate humans
- Make recommendations
- Correlate data
- Much more

## Generative AI Applications

### Core Capabilities
- Helps create, generate, and simulate new content
- Leverages machine learning and deep learning techniques to learn patterns
- Unlike other AI software that follows pre-defined rules
- Enhances application capabilities and experiences
- Acquires knowledge during training
- Used in various fields due to its potential to create new and personalized content

### Applications and Tools

#### Generative Pre-trained Transformers (GPT)
- LLM developed by OpenAI capable of human-like text
- Iterations: GPT-3.5, GPT-4
- **Applications**:
  - Chatbots (ChatGPT)
  - Automated journalism
  - Creative writing

#### ChatGPT
- Advanced language model by OpenAI
- Trained on diverse internet text
- Generates human-like responses
- Offers various suggestions across different subjects

#### Bard (now Gemini)
- Assists in producing high-quality writing
- Generates text using LLM called LaMDA
- Adjusts to the user's style and tone

#### IBM watsonx
- AI and data platform with components:
  - Watsonx.ai - Model development
  - Watsonx.data - Scalable analytics
  - Watsonx.governance - AI workflows
- Helps build, deploy, and manage AI apps

#### DeepDream
- Generates surreal and psychedelic images from real-life images
- Produces visually stunning images for art and entertainment

#### StyleGAN
- Produces high-quality images of unreal faces
- **Applications**:
  - Creating realistic video game avatars
  - Simulating human faces for medical research

#### AlphaFold
- Predicts protein structure
- Transforms drug discovery
- Develops more effective disease treatments

#### Magenta
- Creates music and art using generative AI
- Enables piano duets performed by humans and AI-generated piano

#### PaLM2
- LLM trained on large datasets
- **Capabilities**:
  - Understands nuances
  - Generates coherent texts
  - Translates and answers questions

#### GitHub Copilot
- Assists developers in writing code more efficiently
- Uses deep learning algorithms for:
  - Auto-completing code snippets
  - Creating functions based on code context

### Ethical Concerns
- Potential misuse of AI-generated content
- Implications for intellectual property and copyright laws

### Recap
- Generative AI enables applications to create, generate, and simulate new content
- Leverages ML and deep learning techniques to learn patterns and generate original content
- Applications include GPT-4, ChatGPT, Gemini, GitHub Copilot, and PaLM2
