# Week 6: Troubleshooting

## Troubleshooting Methodology

### Steps of Troubleshooting
- **Definition**: Troubleshooting is the ability to diagnose and resolve problems
- **Key Approaches**:
  - Ask questions to gather information
  - Isolate the problem to identify root cause
  - Follow the "cookie crumbs" by asking when the problem started
  - Start with the quickest steps first

### Troubleshooting Pitfalls to Avoid
1. **Autopilot Mode**: Avoid repeating same steps without considering unique variables
2. **Superficial Solutions**: Failing to find and fix the root cause will not resolve the main problem

## Customer Service in IT Support

### Core Principles
- **Empathy**: Looking at issues from the user's perspective
- **Tone Management**: Maintaining a friendly, positive tone
- **Acknowledgment**: Recognizing the user and their concerns
- **Trust Development**: Building relationships through empathy and acknowledgment

### Anatomy of an Interaction
1. **Initial Contact**: Acknowledge the user professionally and respectfully
2. **Response Process**:
   - Build rapport (remember personal details when appropriate)
   - Clarify the issue before beginning troubleshooting
   - Explain why specific commands or actions are needed
3. **Conclusion**: End on a positive note to set the tone for the entire interaction

### Handling Difficult Situations
- **Personal "Reboot"**: Identify actions that help you regain focus
- **Effective Strategies**:
  - Identify where the interaction went wrong and redirect
  - Understand why the problem is occurring
  - Practice patience
  - Consider the other person's perspective

## Documentation

### Documentation Methods
- **Ticketing Systems**: Common way to document issues
- **Bug Tracking**: Using tools like Bugzilla, JIRA, and Redmine to manage system issues
- **Benefits of Documentation**:
  - Keeps users informed of progress
  - Creates audit trail of troubleshooting steps
  - Provides reference for future issues

### Documentation Best Practices
- **Avoid**: No documentation or insufficient information
- **Include**: Clear, comprehensive information in tickets and process documentation
- **Update**: Maintain current documentation as systems and processes change

## Technical Interview Preparation

### Resume Development
- **Content to Include**:
  - Personal information (name, contact details)
  - Education history
  - Employment history
  - Relevant skills
- **Best Practices**:
  - Use standard templates with consistent formatting
  - Highlight relevant skills for the position
  - Quantify achievements
  - Tailor resume to each job application
  - Maintain professional online presence

### Interview Preparation
- **Practice Techniques**:
  - Rehearse answers out loud
  - Explain technical concepts to others
  - Prepare an "elevator pitch" about yourself
  - Develop explanations for technical concepts

### Technical Interview Expectations
- **Skills Assessment**:
  - Technical troubleshooting scenarios
  - Explanation of technical concepts
  - Problem-solving approach demonstration
- **Strategies**:
  - Show problem-solving methodology rather than just answers
  - Ask clarifying questions
  - Break down complex problems
  - Be prepared to explain technical concepts

### Interview Day Tips
- Get adequate rest
- Eat properly
- Ensure comfort
- Stay fully present
- Ask relevant questions
- Speak at a measured pace
