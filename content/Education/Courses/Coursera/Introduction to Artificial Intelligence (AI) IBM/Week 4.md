# Week 4: The Future of AI and Career Guidance

## The Evolution and Future of AI

### Historical Milestones
- Early AI researchers were interested in games like chess due to their complexity yet well-defined moves/goals
- **Deep Blue**: Leveraged massive compute power to explore more possible chess moves than humans
- **Watson**: Defeated Jeopardy champions by retrieving information from multiple sources and combining it using machine learning

### Current Advancements
- AI has advanced to tackle more ill-defined, "messy" problems across industries
- Industries are now dealing with unstructured multimedia data
- Valuable AI applications in healthcare combine:
  - Image analysis
  - Natural language understanding
  - Patient history analysis to aid diagnosis

### Human-AI Collaboration
- Intersection of AI and human capabilities produces something better than each individually
- Ideal scenario is collaboration between:
  - Machines doing what they excel at
  - Humans doing what they excel at
- Rather than fully autonomous AI, the combination of AI and human intelligence can collectively solve problems better
- Constant search for next "grand challenge" multi-year problem that will provide valuable AI capabilities

## The AI Ladder - The Journey for Adopting AI Successfully

### Business Potential
- Companies are looking to AI to shape the future of work
- AI can:
  - Predict outcomes
  - Enable higher value work
  - Imagine new business models
  - Automate decisions, processes, experiences

### Information Architecture Challenge
- AI requires Information Architecture (IA)
- 80% of data is locked in silos, not business-ready

### The Ladder to AI Framework
| Step | Action | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Modernize** | All data on a single cloud platform |
| 2 | **Collect** | Make data simple, accessible; think about models to train |
| 3 | **Organize** | Create business-ready analytics foundation for AI models |
| 4 | **Analyze** | Ensure trust, transparency; detect bias; prove accuracy |
| 5 | **Infuse** | Operationalize AI throughout the business |

### IBM's Approach
- Helps enterprises unlock data value and put AI to work by:
  - Delivering right skills to people
  - Building trust and transparency into AI
  - Implementing the "Ladder to AI" process

## Advice for a Career in AI

### General Guidance
- AI will permeate every sphere of human endeavor
- Keep an open mind as AI technology moves fast - don't get attached to any particular technique
- Think about what's possible and apply what you learn - make it practical, not just academic

### For Different Experience Levels
- **Beginners**: Take an intro AI course to grasp the basics and find your niche of interest
- **Students**: Focus on math and science which are critical for understanding AI systems
- **Professionals**: Explore academic programs to build the required foundation

### Skill Requirements
- No longer need a PhD - just understand the problem and how to use AI software APIs
- Advanced knowledge still required if building your own AI algorithms
- AI/ML is the future, enabling new ways to interact with computers
- ML is an algorithm in the broader programming toolbox - need solid programming basics first
- Be passionate about technology, learn coding, computational thinking before specializing in ML

### Foundation Building
- AI is built on many foundational fields:
  - Mathematics
  - Statistics
  - Optimization
  - Programming
- Get started quickly by trying simple tasks like digit/object recognition
- It's the "renaissance of software" - get into AI now as it will only keep growing

## Tanmay's Advice to Learn AI

### Common Misconceptions
- Many misconceptions that machine learning (ML) is an isolated subject that can be learned independently
- ML is just another algorithm in the programming toolbox, albeit a powerful one

### Prerequisites
Before getting into ML/AI, it's crucial to:
- Understand how to code and how computers work
- Have advanced knowledge of coding backends, compilers
- ML requires hardware acceleration and understanding calculus behind backpropagation

### Learning Path Recommendations
1. Be passionate about technology itself first
2. Learn core technology concepts and coding (Python, Julia, Swift)
3. Understand the math behind neural networks
4. Practice implementing neural networks from scratch
5. Then use ML libraries/toolkits to rapidly build applications

### For Beginners
- Start with tools like IBM Watson to leverage ML capabilities via APIs
- Get exposure to programming and ML simultaneously
- Then dive deeper into the custom math and modeling
- Key is building solid fundamentals in programming, math, and technology before specializing in ML algorithms
