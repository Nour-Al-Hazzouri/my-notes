# Crafting your Elevator Pitch

## Definition and Purpose
- **What is it**: A short self-description deliverable during an elevator ride
- **Goal**: To explain who you are, what you do, and why others should be interested in you
- **Application**: Useful in interviews, networking events, and professional introductions

## Core Components
- **Personal identification**: Your name
- **Current status or role**: What you currently do
- **Future goals or aspirations**: What you aim to achieve
- **Value proposition**: Why someone should be interested in you

## Contextual Variations

### For Students
- Include what and where you're studying
- Mention post-graduation career goals
- Highlight relevant personal qualities
- **Example**: _"Hi! I'm Jamie, I'm in my senior year at Springfield University, studying Computer Science. I enjoy being able to help people and solve problems, so I'm looking forward to putting my tech skills into practice by working as an IT Support Specialist after I graduate."_

### For Employed Professionals
- Mention current company and position
- Highlight relevant skills acquired
- Specify desired career change
- **Example**: _"Hi! I'm Ellis, I've been working at X Company as an IT Support Specialist for the past two years. During those years, I've learned a lot about operating systems and networking and I'm looking to switch to a system administrator position, where I can focus on large scale deployments."_

## Best Practices
- Keep it personal to create connection
- Capture attention quickly
- Clearly communicate your value
- Be prepared to deliver it in any setting
- Adapt content based on your current career situation
- Maintain brevity while including all essential components
